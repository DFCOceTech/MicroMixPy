# Capability: Turbulence Dissipation Profiles

**Capability ID**: turbulence-dissipation  
**Status**: In Development  
**Last Updated**: 2026-05-03 (REQ-TDISS-007, REQ-TDISS-008 added)

---

## Requirements

### REQ-TDISS-001 — Epsilon via Nasmyth spectral fitting over fixed 5–40 cpm band
Epsilon SHALL be estimated per depth bin by fitting the Nasmyth (1970) universal
shear spectrum to the observed shear PSD over the fixed wavenumber band 5 ≤ k ≤ 40 cpm
using log-space least-squares minimisation.  The fixed band avoids low-frequency ship/
mooring contamination (< 5 cpm) and the viscous sub-range (> 40 cpm).

### REQ-TDISS-002 — Chi via temperature gradient PSD integration
Chi SHALL be estimated per depth bin by integrating the calibrated FP07 temperature
gradient PSD: chi = 6 * kappa_T * integral(phi_dT/dz dk).

### REQ-TDISS-003 — Dual-probe best estimate
Both shear probes (sh1, sh2) SHALL be processed independently. A best-estimate epsilon
SHALL be derived as the element-wise minimum of eps1 and eps2.

### REQ-TDISS-004 — Near-surface exclusion for ship-generated turbulence
`compute_epsilon_profile` and `compute_chi_profile` SHALL accept an
`exclude_above_dbar` parameter (default 0.0). Depth bins whose center pressure is
≤ `exclude_above_dbar` SHALL be set to NaN and excluded from the profile. This
allows the user to mask turbulence generated by the ship's hull, which can be
indistinguishable from oceanic turbulence spectrally.

### REQ-TDISS-005 — Chi via Batchelor spectrum fitting (default)
`compute_chi_profile` SHALL support two methods via a `method` parameter:

- **`'batchelor'` (default)**: Fit the Batchelor (1959) temperature gradient spectrum
  to the observed PSD to jointly estimate chi and an independent epsilon (eps_batchelor).
  Uses the Oakey (1982) spectral shape with q = 3.7.
  Formula: Φ_B(k) = chi/(6·κ_T·k_B) · 2q·(k/k_B)·exp(−q·(k/k_B)²)
  where k_B = (ε/(ν·κ_T²))^(1/4)/(2π) is the Batchelor wavenumber.
  Fitting is performed in log-space over (chi, eps) using bounded minimization.

- **`'direct'`**: Integrate the observed PSD: chi = 6·κ_T·∫Φ dk (prior behaviour).

The return signature SHALL be `(depth, chi, eps_batchelor)`.
`eps_batchelor` is NaN for the `'direct'` method.

### REQ-TDISS-006 — Batchelor eps reliability flag
When the fitted Batchelor wavenumber k_B exceeds 50% of the Nyquist wavenumber,
`eps_batchelor` for that bin SHALL be set to NaN (spectral rolloff not observed).
chi is still returned if the fit converged.

### REQ-TDISS-007 — Profiler deceleration flag at end of downcast
`extract_downcasts` SHALL flag samples at the **end** of the downcast where the
smoothed fall speed drops below 90 % of the median (terminal) fall speed. These
samples SHALL be combined with the acceleration flag (start-of-profile) and passed
to `compute_epsilon_profile` and `compute_chi_profile` as the `accel_flag` mask, so
that bins containing tether-tension / deceleration-contaminated shear are excluded
from epsilon and chi estimates. The symptom being prevented is an artefactual spike
in ε at the deepest bin(s) caused by profiler vibration as the tether goes taut.

### REQ-TDISS-008 — Thorpe scale computation tolerates minor pressure reversals
`compute_thorpe_scales` SHALL NOT silently skip profiles with small non-monotonic
depth values (e.g., from sensor noise or attitude wobble at segment boundaries).
Instead it SHALL sort the valid-sample arrays by depth before calling mixsea, then
inverse-sort the results back to the original index order.

## Scenarios

### SCENARIO-TDISS-004-A — Excluded bins are NaN
Given a downcast processed with `exclude_above_dbar=10`,
When the returned epsilon profile is inspected,
Then all bins with center depth ≤ 10 dbar SHALL be NaN.

### SCENARIO-TDISS-004-B — Bins below threshold are unaffected
Given a downcast processed with `exclude_above_dbar=10`,
When the returned epsilon profile is inspected,
Then bins with center depth > 10 dbar SHALL be identical to the
result with `exclude_above_dbar=0`.

### SCENARIO-TDISS-005-A — Batchelor recovers known chi from synthetic spectrum
Given a synthetic Batchelor spectrum with known chi and eps,
When fit_batchelor is applied,
Then the recovered chi is within 0.5 log-decades of the true value.

### SCENARIO-TDISS-005-B — Batchelor eps is NaN above Nyquist cutoff
Given a depth bin where the Batchelor wavenumber exceeds 50% of Nyquist,
When compute_chi_profile is called with method='batchelor',
Then eps_batchelor for that bin SHALL be NaN.

### SCENARIO-TDISS-007-A — Deceleration-phase samples are flagged
Given a smoothed fall-speed array that ramps from terminal velocity to zero over
the last 20 % of samples,
When `_deceleration_flag` is called,
Then the last 20 % of samples SHALL be True and the first 80 % SHALL be False.

### SCENARIO-TDISS-007-B — Mid-profile samples are not flagged during steady fall
Given a smoothed fall-speed array that is constant at terminal velocity,
When `_deceleration_flag` is called,
Then all samples SHALL be False (no samples flagged).

### SCENARIO-TDISS-008-A — Thorpe scales computed despite minor non-monotonicity
Given a stably-stratified density profile whose depth array has a single-sample
reversal (< 0.1 dbar),
When `compute_thorpe_scales` is called,
Then it SHALL return all-zero Thorpe scales (stable profile, correct result) rather
than silently returning zeros due to the non-monotonicity guard.
